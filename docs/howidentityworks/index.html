How Digital Identity Works

Digital identity offers an optional way to access government services.

Users can choose to create a digital identity to prove who they are. They can use their digital identity to sign in to the services they need to use.

Video here

1. The user chooses a provider to create their digital identity

If a user wants a digital identity they choose an identity service provider to create and manage it:

  1. myGovID (operated by the Australian Taxation Office)
  2. Digital iD (operated by Australia Post)

There will be more identity service providers to choose from in the future, including commercial ones.

2. Digital identity providers check the user’s documents

To create their digital identity, the user scans documents using the identity provider’s app (for example, an iPhone app).

A photo on a Driver's License would be checked through the Document Verification Service with the state or territory road authority.

The identity provider service will access different verification services. This is based on what level of identity the user is applying for and the documents they provide.

After the scans have been checked the identity provider creates the digital identity. This is stored on the user’s mobile or tablet.

3. The user signs in using a code from their device

The user requests a code from the app on their mobile or tablet. They enter this code on their computer to sign into the service they need to use.

If they need to do something else that requires identification, they can use their digital identity and complete the task without having to show their documents again.

Example of a user accessing government services with digital identity

A user wants to apply for a payment while they are studying.

The user downloads the myGovID app and creates a digital identity by scanning their driver’s license and passport.

They sign in to myGov on their computer using a code from their myGovID app on their mobile. They can apply for the JobSeeker payment, which is run by Services Australia.

The same user needs to do their first tax return later. They can sign in to myGov using their digital identity and lodge their tax return online.

They do not need to repeat the process to prove who they are to the Australian Taxation Office.

Digital identity is managed through a federation

Different systems run by agencies and businesses work together to provide digital identity. It is not a single identification number in a government database.

Agencies can join the federation and use digital identity without having to set up their own identity systems. It means users get a choice about how to prove their identity to government and enhanced privacy if they chose to create a digital identity.
