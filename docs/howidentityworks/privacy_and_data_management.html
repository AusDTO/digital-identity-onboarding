Privacy and data management

We work with members of the federation, businesses, non-government organisations and the public to put privacy first.

Digital identity is based on strong protection but there are always risks.

The federation uses the OpenID protocol with additional protection including encryption, making it hard to hack. But there are things we cannot prevent against (for example, someone stealing a user’s phone and login details).

The exchange hub helps protect privacy

The digital identity ecosystem uses several systems to protect privacy. One of the systems is the exchange hub, which is run by Services Australia.

The hub prevents the agency who runs the service from seeing how the user proved their identity.

The same system prevents the identity service provider from seeing which service the person is using and any of the details of their interaction.

Services access limited information

Users can choose to share details (attributes) with services to use them:

  • family name
  • given names
  • date of birth
  • email address
  • phone number
  • authority to act on behalf of a business

If you have a need for other document details you can apply to the oversight authority. To do this you must have a legislative need and the user would need to agree to supply these details.

You will be able to trust that the attributes your service receives are verified.

You could apply to ask for a driver's license number. A person could then use digital identity to prove who they are and renew their driver's license online.

Systems are accredited

Independent organisations use the Trusted Digital Identity Framework (TDIF) to accredit parts of the digital identity ecosystem:

  • identity service providers are accredited to allow then to create a digital identity (for example, myGovID)
  • the exchange hub is accredited so it can protect privacy when it passes interactions from the user to the service
  • attribute providers are accredited so they can verify parts of identity documents (for example, a user’s tax file number)
  • credential service providers are accredited so they can allow digital identity to be reused by the user.

These parts meet government requirements, including:

  • independent Information Security Registered Assessors Program (IRAP) assessment
  • complying with the Information Security Manual and Protective Security Policy Framework penetration testing

The system has undergone 2 privacy impact assessments.

Services do not need to be accredited to create and run a service (for example, applying for money while looking for work). Your agency should make sure they meet the Digital Service Standard.

Please contact us if you need more information on our federation privacy work.

Minimum data is stored

The digital identity systems store just the data needed to run and improve the service:

  • identity providers (for example, myGovID) store the information allowed by the user to prove their identity
  • exchange hub stores when and how information passes through it but no personal information (attributes)
