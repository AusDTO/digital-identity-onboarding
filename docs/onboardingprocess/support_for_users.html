User support for your service

Some users may have trouble with parts of your service. Your agency is responsible for helping them to complete their transaction.

Plan how you will support users

You need to plan how you will fund and run your support team.

You will also have to plan how to forward requests for help to organisations that run other parts of the digital identity ecosystem (for example, an identity provider).

You should make this a smooth experience for the user. They should be able to use the service without understanding the digital identity ecosystem.

Some agencies in the digital identity federation refer to the users of services as ‘customers’ or ‘clients’.

Support users to get help in different ways

When a user gets stuck in a service they may look for help in different ways.

Your agency should plan how it will support a user:

  • phoning a helpdesk or call centre
  • searching and browsing website content, including where they download apps (App Store or Google Store)
  • sending an email to a support address
  • talking to a chatbot
  • visiting a government office or shopfront

You may not own some of these channels, so you need to plan to make it easy for the user who moves between them.

Example of a user seeking help

A user of a government service might be worried about privacy and ask the service chatbot “Why do I need to give my passport?”.

The agency who owns the service needs to make sure the chatbot can provide the answer or provide a link in the answer to the agency that can answer the question.

Support people who don’t use digital identity

Digital identity is an opt-in platform to help people prove their identity and authenticate to access more government services online.

You must make your service available to all users. This includes people who do not want to or cannot use digital identity.

You need to provide alternative ways for people to identify themselves to use your service.

You will also need to plan how you will make sure people with disability and people who struggle with digital services can access the service.

Handle complaints using your agency’s process

You should follow your agency process for handling complaints with your service. A complaints process is a mandatory requirement for all government organisations.

If the user is complaining about part of the digital identity ecosystem that you do not own (for example, an identity provider or exchange) you should send the complaint to [contact details].

We will forward the complaint to the right team.
