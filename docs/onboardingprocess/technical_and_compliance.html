Technical and Compliance

To onboard your service to digital identity, you will need to sign a memorandum of understanding, and make sure you can meet technical and fraud protection requirements.

Signing a memorandum of understanding with us

The senior responsible officer in your agency will need to sign a memorandum of understanding (MOU).

This agreement sets out the detailed requirements you will need to follow as part of the federation. It is based on the Trusted Digital Identity Framework (TDIF), which governs digital identity.

The MOU will set out the service level agreements for your service.

We are developing standard service level agreements for the TDIF.

Work out what level of identity proofing you need

There are 4 levels of identity proofing.

You can request the identity proofing level the service needs based on how confident your agency needs to be that the user is who they say they are.

You should work out the risk of fraud in your service to help you decide what level you need.

It’s easier and cheaper for agencies and the user if you set the identity level for your service as low as possible. Many government services only need level 2 to provide a service.

Digital identity helps users prove their identity up to level 3. If your service needs level 4 and above please book a meeting with our team.

Identity proofing level Amount of confidence in user's identity needed for the service Example of an interaction that may need this level What the user needs to do to get this level
Level 1 Low confidence Pay a parking ticket or get a fishing license. The user just needs to say who they are.
Level 2 Medium confidence (similar to a 100-point check) Apply for an Australian Business Number (ABN) Show 2 or more documents.
Level 3 High confidence Apply for a Medicare card. Show 3 or more documents and get checked against a photo.
Level 4 Very high confidence Apply for a passport. The level 3 requirements and an in-person interview.

Identity proofing levels are like levels of assurance, but the requirements to get them are different. Digital identity uses identity proofing levels to make it easier for everyone in the federation to work together.

Understand the technical requirements

The TDIF sets out the technical requirements for digital identity. Digital identity uses the OpenID Connect 1.0 protocol. This allows your service to communicate with the exchange hub.

The TDIF has specifications for using the SAML 2.0 protocol. This may be able to be implemented by the federation if services need it.

It will be easier to onboard a new service already using these protocols than building a new service or adapting one that just collects a username and password.

Book a meeting with us if you need more technical help.

Secure your service

Your service must comply with government standards to protect against fraud, including having a disaster management plan in place.

This means you are responsible for investigating fraud that only affects your service. This might be fraud based on extra attributes only your service needs (for example, a driver’s license number).

If there is a data breach that includes personal information you must tell your users.

You can read guidance on how to respond to a data breach.

Fraud that affects the federation

The DTA will tell you if we find fraud that affects the federation.

If you suspect fraud or a find a data breach that may affect other services you must tell the DTA.

The DTA is the oversight authority and manages fraud response for the federation. If there is enough evidence of fraud and risk, we can:

  • suspend the suspected identity
  • allow your agency to receive transaction information
  • allow you to contact the identity provider to investigate

The federation’s fraud working group is developing standards for fraud management.
